# Shared core (cross-profile rules)

Rules that hold across every kind of writing I do, not just one genre. Every profile's skill reads this file in addition to its own style guide, and audits every draft against it. This is the public analog of a "where learnings go" routing table: a rule lives in exactly one place, and universal rules live here so they don't have to be relearned in each profile.

Copy this file to the root of your voice-data folder (e.g. `~/writing-voice/shared-core.md`). Each generated profile points at it automatically.

## How rules arrive here

Two paths, both proposed by the skill during a profile's learning step (never silent):

1. **Looks universal now.** A promotion that is plainly format or mechanics level (punctuation, spacing, a tell that has nothing to do with the genre) gets offered to shared-core instead of that profile's local scrub list.
2. **Promoted in two profiles.** When the skill goes to promote a rule and finds the same rule already promoted in another profile, that is strong evidence it is universal. It offers to consolidate: move the rule here and leave a one-line pointer (`see shared-core`) in each local list.

Genre-specific rules never come here. "Warm-ups earn their place by retrieval" belongs to lesson scripts; "no em dashes" belongs to everyone.

## Scrub list (audited on every draft, every profile)

- Em dashes and en dashes: use a period, comma, parentheses, or a new sentence.

## Sourcing

The AI-tell catalog (`ai-writing-forbidden-patterns.md`) is a separate, larger reference of patterns to avoid in formal writing; it is not this file. Shared-core holds the smaller set of rules *your own corrections* have proven universal for *you*. Catalog = general knowledge; shared-core = your learned cross-profile voice.
